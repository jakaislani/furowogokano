    Career

    In 1957, he began his engineering career with Cessna Aircraft Company, where he worked on the Cessna CH-1 Skyhook four-seat helicopter. He spent one year working on the certification of the Umbaugh U-17 after 3+12 years at Cessna and 4+12 years at McCulloch Aircraft Corporation completing design studies on low-cost rotorcraft. Robinson worked on gyrodyne-type rotorcraft for a year at Kaman Aircraft. He then spent two years at Bell Helicopter, where he became known as a “tail rotor expert.” He joined Hughes Helicopters in 1969 to work on a range of research and development projects, including a redesigned tail rotor for the Hughes 500 helicopter and work on the “The Quiet One” program.

    Frank Robinson announced his resignation as President and Chairman of Robinson Helicopter Company on August 10, 2010. Robinson had planned to retire in January 2010 when he was 80, but he decided to wait until the design of the Robinson R66 Turbine was finished. Robinson opted to call it quits with R66 production underway and FAA certification on the horizon. On August 10, 2010, his son Kurt Robinson was elected by the Board of Directors to take over as President and Chairman.
