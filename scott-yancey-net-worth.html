    What is Scott Yancey’s net worth?

    Scott Yancey, a Las Vegas-based home flipper, author, teacher, and reality television star, has a combined net worth of $20 million with his wife, Amie Yancey. He is well-known for his work on the A&E television show “Flipping Vegas”.

    Early Life and Education

    Scott E. Yancy was born on July 9, 1969, in Los Angeles, California and grew up in nearby Studio City. He attended North Hollywood High School where he graduated in 1987.

    Scott Yancey’s Real Estate Journey

    Scott Yancey’s interest in real estate began at the age of 14 when he bought a small second trust deed on a home in Studio City, California. During college, he worked with real estate attorney and investor, Walter J. Plumb, and assisted him in several transactions, including the purchase of an apartment complex with 40 units which they converted into upscale condos.

    After developing department stores around Southwest states, Yancey returned to real estate in 1994 when he moved to Las Vegas. He started flipping houses and gained momentum after discovering that houses in Las Vegas could be bought for under $50,000. Today, Yancey is the owner and managing partner of Goliath Company, which owns and manages many income properties around Las Vegas. He buys old houses, refurbishes them, and either sells them for a profit or rents them to tenants. With over 20 years of experience in the industry, Yancey has become a successful real estate investor.

    Flipping Vegas and Yancey Events

    In 2010, Yancey teamed up with Lovable Scoundrels Productions to create the A&E TV series “Flipping Vegas.” The show’s first episode premiered on June 18, 2011, and it ran for five seasons until September 2014. Yancey served as both the host and executive producer of the show.

    Yancey and his wife Amie also founded Yancey Events, which hosts educational seminars on real estate investing across the country.

    Scott Yancey’s Multi-Faceted Career

    When not working long hours, Scott Yancey can be found racing his Porsche on West Coast tracks. He is also a published author, with his books “Go Time” and “Flipping Your Way to Real Estate Profits.” Yancey’s education business has over 250,000 students annually, while he has also developed retail stores in Las Vegas, Tempe, and Provo.

    Personal Life

    Yancey and Amie tied the knot in January 2000 and share a love for animals. They have three dogs, two horses, two pigs, and a mini donkey.

    Scott Yancey also deals with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).
