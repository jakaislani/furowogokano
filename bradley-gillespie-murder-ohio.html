    Bradley Gillespie Murder: Dangerous Inmates Escape Ohio Prison

    Bradley Gillespie murder escape has sparked a statewide manhunt. Ohio authorities are searching for two inmates who escaped the Allen/Oakwood Correctional Institution in Lima. Bradley Gillespie, 50, and James Lee, 47, were reported missing on Tuesday, prompting a massive search.

    28 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Bradley Gillespie murder escape has sparked a statewide manhunt.

    Ohio authorities are searching for two inmates who escaped the Allen/Oakwood Correctional Institution in Lima. 

    Bradley Gillespie, 50, and James Lee, 47, were reported missing on Tuesday, prompting a massive search. 

    The Ohio Department of Rehabilitation and Correction and the Ohio State Highway Patrol have warned that both men should be considered dangerous, urging the public not to approach them but to contact 911 if they are spotted immediately.

    Gillespie and Lee escape has raised concerns among law enforcement and the local community. 

    Gillespie had been serving a sentence for murder since 2016, while Lee was incarcerated for burglary and safecracking, having arrived at the prison in 2021. 

    The authorities have not disclosed the exact details of their escape, leaving many questions regarding the circumstances surrounding their disappearance.

    The investigation

    The Ohio Department of Rehabilitation and Correction has launched an internal investigation to determine how Gillespie and Lee managed to escape undetected. The focus now is on locating the two fugitives swiftly and safely, returning them to custody. 

    As the search intensifies, residents are being urged to remain vigilant and report any sightings or information related to the whereabouts of Gillespie and Lee to the authorities.

    Law enforcement agencies, including the Ohio State Highway Patrol, have taken the lead in the investigation, coordinating efforts to apprehend the escaped inmates. 

    The seriousness of their crimes, particularly Bradley Gillespie murder conviction, underscores their potential danger to the public. 

    James Lee was convicted of burglary, breaking and entering, and safecracking in Allen and Auglaize Counties.

    Anyone with relevant information is encouraged to come forward to assist in capturing the fugitives.

    The Allen/Oakwood Correctional Institution and local law enforcement agencies are working closely to coordinate search efforts and establish a comprehensive plan for the inmates’ recapture. 

    Cooperation between these entities is crucial in ensuring a swift and successful resolution.

    Bradley Gillespie’s conviction stems from the 2016 murders of Frank Tracy, Jr. and Hannah Fisher in Paulding County. 

    Tracy’s body was discovered near a cemetery, while Fisher’s was found in an apartment. The investigation revealed that both victims had been shot in the head inside a red Jeep. 

    Gillespie’s escape raises concerns about public safety, considering the violent nature of his previous offenses.

    On the other hand, James Lee had been serving time for burglary and safecracking charges. 

    His criminal history includes a guilty plea for burglary in Allen County in 2021 and a conviction for breaking and entering in Auglaize County in 2020. 

    While his offenses may not be as severe as Gillespie’s, his escape is still a matter of significant concern for law enforcement.

    As the search for Gillespie and Lee continues, authorities rely on public tips and leads to aid in their apprehension.  

    The timely reporting of sightings or any relevant details could play a crucial role in the efforts to locate and secure the escaped inmates.

    Communities in and around Lima are advised to exercise caution and remain aware of their surroundings until Gillespie and Lee are safely back in custody. 

    The impact of their escape extends beyond the prison walls, instilling a sense of unease and prompting increased security measures. 

    Authorities are working diligently to ensure public safety and swiftly resolve this situation, emphasizing the importance of cooperation and vigilance from law enforcement and the community at large.
