    Reagan Lancaster receives IFBB/NPC award from Ed and Betty Pariso at the Dallas Europa

    DALLAS, June 27, 2018 /PRNewswire/ --On June 17, 2018, Reagan Lancaster was given an award in recognition and support of the IFBB/NPC Texas by Ed &amp; Betty Pariso, Chairpeople of the IFBB/NPC (International Federation for Bodybuilding and the National Physique Committee), at the Kay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center in Dallas, Texas.

    27 Jun 2024, 00:00

    DALLAS, June 27, 2018 /PRNewswire/ -- On June 17, 2018, Reagan Lancaster was given an award in recognition and support of the IFBB/NPC Texas by Ed & Betty Pariso, Chairpeople of the IFBB/NPC (International Federation for Bodybuilding and the National Physique Committee), at the Kay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center in Dallas, Texas.

    Lancaster competed in two shows for 2017, and in the process of prepping for these shows, broke his leg and blew out his ACL in a motorcycle accident. Well known knee doctor (Sports Medicine, Knee/ACL Surgery, Texas Sports Medicine), Tarek Souryal who had been the Dallas Mavericks team doctor and consultant to the NFL, told Reagan that he could get him through the shows. The fracture in the tibia had enough bone to safely compete, but the knee and swelling had to be dealt with to see if it was possible to ride a bike for cardio. With a de-mobilizer fitted to the leg, Lancaster had 3 drainings of the knee and had a strong will to compete. While at the emergency room in Plano Baylor Hospital following the accident, he said there was no way he could make it. Thankfully, "With the help of good doctors and several visits to the hospital I was able to make the shows for the year," reported Lancaster. He was then able to ride a bike for his last 2 weeks, helping him to come in lean enough to compete.

    Steve Kuclo (IFBB professional and world-class bodybuilder), who gave the award to Reagan said, "I have never seen anything like that before," and called Lancaster a warrior. Being an athlete in the bodybuilding community and coming in with a great heart and support to the entire league in Texas, Ed Pariso and Betty wanted to honor him and his wife Lisa Lancaster with an award for their perseverance and support for the sport.

    Reagan Lancaster has been an accomplished businessman in the Dallas area and has competed in 11 competitions in the last 4 years as a bodybuilder. He is also an avid rodeo and horseman with winnings over $450,000, still competes on horses, and owns Lancaster Ranch.

    If you'd like to know more about Reagan Lancaster, you can follow him at www.reaganlancaster.net or find him on social media: Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, Google Plus, or LinkedIn.
